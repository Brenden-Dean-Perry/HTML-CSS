Article vs Section <article> & <section>

Article and Section are both semantic elements in HTML, but they serve different purposes.
An Article is a self-contained piece of content that can be distributed independently, such as a blog post or news article. A Section, on the other hand, is a thematic grouping of content within a document, such as a chapter or a section of a webpage. While an Article can contain multiple Sections, a Section cannot contain an Article.

Aside <aside>

An aside is a section of content that is tangentially related to the content around it. It is often used for sidebars, pull quotes, or other content that is not essential to the main content but provides additional information or context. In this case, the aside is providing additional information about the difference between Article and Section elements in HTML.

Semantic Metadata

Semantic metadata provides additional information about the content, helping search engines and assistive technologies understand the context and meaning of the content. For example:

Span and Div <span> & <div>

The <span> element is an inline container used to mark up a part of a text, or a part of a document. The <div> element is a block-level container used to group elements for styling or scripting purposes.

These use to be the only way to group elements before semantic elements were introduced, but now they are typically used for styling or scripting purposes rather than for structuring content. In fact, we should avoid using <div> and <span> for structuring content, as it can make the HTML document less semantic and harder to understand for both humans and machines.
